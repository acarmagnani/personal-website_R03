Louis Sullivan used to say that form follows function, but this wine cellar shows that both can occur simultaneously.
In this project, an amorphous wooden panel embraces a living room for a house in Singapore, delineating spaces and creating divisions. It is from this element that a wine cellar for more than 5,000 bottles emerges through pins that are extruded from the panel, being positioned parametrically.
This project was developed at the internationally renowned architecture firm Isay Weinfeld, based in SÃ£o Paulo. It consists on a single-family residence in Singapore elaborated by a team of architects, namely Enzo Tos, Lucas Jimeno Dualde, Eduardo Lisboa and Leonardo Botene, in addition to myself. For this project, I had the role of modeling the work and assisting in its conception.
The entire structure of this project was modelled parametrically using grasshopper.
